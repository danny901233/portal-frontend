The three routes01

There are three ways in Two are self-serve and pay by card. One is sold on a demo call and pays by Direct Debit. The route decides who chases them, how they pay, and whether they sign anything.

Assist £200 + VAT / month · £240 charged

AI receptionist answering calls. The garage finds themselves on /get-started, signs online, adds a card, and gets 14 days free. 400 minutes included, then 25p a minute.

Finds their garage→ Signs online→ Adds card→ 14-day trial→ Charged, live
Connect £250 + VAT / month · £300 charged

Website chat. The garage signs up on /mot with a code texted to their mobile, and is inside the portal within a minute — no card, nothing signed. 30 days free, then a paywall asks for a card. 500 conversations included, then 20p each.

Enters details→ Texted a code→ Straight in, 30 days free→ Paywall→ Adds card, live
Automate Priced per deal · Direct Debit

The full integration with Garage Hive or Tyresoft — books jobs straight into their diary. Always starts with a demo call. We set the price, send an agreement, build the agent, then invite them in. Optional one-off setup fee.

Demo call→ You onboard + send agreement→ They sign + pay setup fee→ You build the agent→ You invite→ Direct Debit, live
The rule that decides everything: self-serve always pays by card (Stripe). Anything we sell on a demo call pays by Direct Debit unless you deliberately change it. A customer is on one rail or the other — never card for one thing and Direct Debit for another.

Things that catch people out08

Read this bit None of these are broken exactly. They're just the places where what you'd assume and what actually happens part company.

Older garages may be in HighLevel twice

Until July 2026 signup filed a contact against the Google phone before it knew an email, while the “talk to us” form filed one against the email — and nothing joined them. Those garages are in there twice, sometimes with the money on the one without an email. New signups can't do this; the old ones are still there. When the portal asks you to pick an opportunity, read the list.

Nothing is recorded until they hand over an email

Browsing, searching for your garage, typing half a form — none of it reaches HighLevel. An opportunity exists only once someone submits an email, which is also the first moment they're worth chasing.

HighLevel failing is silent

Every update we send is fire-and-forget. If HighLevel is down or the record has gone, nothing throws, nothing retries and nobody's told — the portal carries on and the CRM quietly drifts. The portal is always the truth; HighLevel is a convenience.

A garage with no linked opportunity is invisible

Skip the dropdown at Quick Onboard and everything still works — the agreement, the invoice, going live — but not one of it appears in HighLevel. You can link it later on the pipeline card.

Signing an Assist deal isn't a sale

The card creates the account, and we check with Stripe that one really exists before building anything — signing alone gets nobody in. Someone who signed and never added a card has a contract on file, no account, and an opportunity still in Abandoned checkout. A sales chase, not a support problem.

“Invited” is where money goes to die

An Automate garage in Invited has a login and a working agent, and no way of paying us. Nothing chases them automatically. Watch that stage.

The setup fee is due on signing, but gates nothing

Agreement and invoice both say due on signing — no 14-day terms. But nothing stops an unpaid customer: the agent still gets built, they still go live. Deliberate, so a slow accounts department can't stall a build — which means nobody chases it but you.
